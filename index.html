VECTORS AND SCALARS

scalar->physical quantity with magnitude only(e.g Distance,mass,time,charge etc).

MOTION IN ONE DIMENSION

Motion->Change in position of an object with respect to time.
frame of referrence->A set of referrence points that enable the position of an object to be defined at any time.(i.e N,E,S,W)
Distance-> Total length of path travelled regardless of the direction.
Speed-> total distance cover over time.
Displacement-> Change in an object's position
Velocity-> Rate of change in displacement per unit time.
accelaration-> Rate of change in velocity per unit time.
  • Acceleration tells us how velocity is changing.
    • N.B: Positive accelaration means that the object's speed is increasing. Negative acceleration means that the the object's speed is decreasing(slowing down)
Uniform velocity-> motion with zero or no acceleration(i.e costant velocity)
uniform acceleration-> when velocity changes with the same amount during each time interval.
Instantaneous Velocity-> displacement divided by a very small time interval(i.e velocity at a particular point) ->the gradient of the tangent of a point on the position vs time graph.

Graphs of motion for uniform velocity

  • Uniform velocity=zero accelaration,the position of the objects changes at equal intervals(the graph is linear).
  • The area under the velocity vs time graph equals the change of position.(i.e depending on the shape, A=L*B)

Graphs of motion for uniform accelaration

  • Uniform accelaration = velocity changes in equal intervals(i.e the velocity vs time graph is straight line)
  • The gradient of the velocity vs time graph equals the accelaration.
  • The area under the velocity vs time graph equals the displacement
  • position vs time graph is convex.
  • The gradient of the tangent of the position vs Time graphs equals the Instantaneous velocity

Vehicles

Breaking distance-> Shortest distance that it takes the brakes of a vehicle to bring it to stop.
Reation time-> Time between realizing that there has beeen a change in the traffic and doing something about it.
Thinking distance-> Distance that a vehicle travels before the driver applies the breaks.
Stopping distance-> The total distance needed to stop a motor vehicle.(i.e Stopping Distance=Breaking distance + Thinking Distance)
